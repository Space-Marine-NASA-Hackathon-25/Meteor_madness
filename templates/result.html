Consequences Of Collision

Air Explosion

Your meteor exploded in the air at an altitude of 2 km. If you were farther than 10 km from the epicenter, you would have remained unharmed. At shorter distances, shock waves, broken windows, and minor building damage are possible.

Ground Impact

The meteor reached the Earth's surface, creating an impact crater about 300 meters in diameter and 60 meters deep. Within a 5 km radius, everything would be destroyed by the shock wave.

Seismic Effects

Ground tremors could be felt even up to 20 km away. The fall of the meteorite would shake the ground like a magnitude 5.3 earthquake.

Tsunami Impact

The meteor fell into a body of water, generating a wave 40 meters high near the epicenter. At the shore, the wave height reached 5 meters.

Safety Zones

If you are farther than 20 km from the shore, the effects would be minimal. Coastal areas could experience partial or complete flooding. If you are near the shore, the effects would be critical.
